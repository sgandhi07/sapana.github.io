Energizing NYC

A couple map enthusiasts and I researched, conceptualized, and designed this map as part of "Atlas in a Day Challenge" hosted by Guerrilla Cartography Collaborative. The theme of "Migration" was announced at the beginning of day, and the challenege was to submit a map by end of the day; and below is our submission. I primarily supported the design part of the challenge by selecting the color scheme and scales.

When brainstorming, we thought of three large types of migrations: human; natural (water bodies, wildlife, etc,.); and capital, goods, and resources. Furthermore, as a group, we are interested in urban infrastructure, and decided to map the upcoming shift in origin of energy consumed by NYC because it addressed the ease at which resources cross political boundries (juxtaposing the difficulty of humans to migrate into the United States).

Moreover, the inset shows the land as indigenous territory to highlight the contentious nature of contemporary political borders, and to situate the idea of migration within that framework.
