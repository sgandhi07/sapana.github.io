In 2020, during the pandemic, I became drawn to my parent's under-utilized backyard.

My family had been expressing interest in gardening. I used the lockdown time to observe and map sun path; note local insect and plant ecology in the nearby woods; and research suitable vegetable, fruit, and ecologicall benefical plantings. Along with edible plants, my family really enjoyed roses, and other flora.

At each stage of the process, I will take stock of what works and what doesn't; especially taking into account feedback from my mother, the primary steward.

This year (2021), I am looking forward to sourcing native, perennials so that the plantings provide ecosystem services and maintenance is kept to a minimum. Moreover, I am exploring the feasibility of installing a rain garden and/or rain barrel(s) in the backyard.
